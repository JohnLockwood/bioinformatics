## AI Response
Before diving into the mechanics of transcription, we need to untangle a slight mix-up in the gene names, as it is incredibly easy to swap them when dealing with this much specialized vocabulary!

### Clarifying the Genes: GABBR2 vs. GABRA2
Severe, early-onset epileptic encephalopathy (such as DEE59) is associated with pathological mutations in the ***GABBR2*** gene. This gene encodes a subunit of the slow-acting, metabotropic GABA-B receptor. Because this specific subunit links the receptor to the internal G-protein that suppresses cellular activity, broken structural mutations here severely disrupt the brain's ability to inhibit electrical signals, leading directly to the over-excitation seen in early-onset seizures. 

***GABRA2***, on the other hand, encodes the alpha-2 subunit of the fast-acting GABA-A receptor. While variations in *GABRA2* are strongly linked to Alcohol Use Disorder (AUD), they are not the structural mutations driving epileptic encephalopathy.

### The Mechanism of AUD Susceptibility
You are exactly right about the nature of the *GABRA2* variants linked to alcoholism. The SNPs most strongly associated with AUD sit in the intronic and intergenic regulatory regions, acting as "volume dials" rather than changing the physical protein structure of the receptor itself. Because there are no mutations in the coding sequence (exons) of the high-risk variants, researchers hypothesized that these SNPs influence gene regulation by altering how much of the alpha-2 subunit is actually produced.

Your assumption that susceptibility is tied to downregulated receptor production via limited transcription is **demonstrated**, not just speculative.

Recent biological research has empirically validated this hypothesis:
* **Reduced mRNA Expression:** Studies utilizing human induced pluripotent stem cell (iPSC)-derived neural cultures have proven that *GABRA2* risk variants act as expression quantitative trait loci (eQTLs). Neural cells carrying the risk alleles demonstrate significantly lower levels of *GABRA2* mRNA transcription compared to those with protective alleles [[1]](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4558268/). 
* **Epigenetic Silencing:** Research indicates that these regulatory variations correlate with altered DNA methylation at the *GABRA2* promoter. Increased methylation essentially tightens the DNA structure, physically limiting the cellular machinery's ability to transcribe the gene, which leads to the exact downregulated receptor production you described [[2]](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8022112/).


Because these regulatory SNPs subtly change the density or availability of GABA-A receptors, they permanently shift the brain's baseline level of neural excitation and inhibition.
